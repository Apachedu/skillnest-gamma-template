Research Resources

Find credible sources, collect data, cite correctly, and structure your IB EE/IA.

Find papers & reviews

  • Google Scholar — broad academic search; use quotes and year filters.
  • JSTOR and DOAJ — journals incl. open access.
  • RePEc/IDEAS — economics working papers.
  • SSRN — management/finance preprints.
Tip: search like "carbon tax" India site:oecd.org 2018..2024 PDF or use intitle:review for survey articles.

Official data (Economics/BM)

  • World Bank Data, OECD Stats, IMF, UN Data
  • India: MoSPI, RBI, SEBI
  • Markets: NSE, BSE
  • Consumer/tech: company annual reports, investor presentations, Statista (if school has access)
Always note: source, series name, units, frequency, and date downloaded.

Primary data (quick guide)

  • Surveys: 8–15 well-designed questions, pilot test with 3–5 people, avoid double-barrel items.
  • Sampling: describe who, where, how many, and how selected.
  • Interviews: 6–10 semi-structured questions; record permission and anonymise.

Citation & bibliography

  • Use one style consistently (APA or MLA recommended by many IB schools).
  • Zotero or Google Docs “Explore” can auto-format references—still check capitalization and DOI/URL.

APA in-text: (Surname, Year). APA reference (journal):

Author, A. A. (Year). Title of article. Journal Name, 12(3), 45–60. https://doi.org/xx.xxxx/xxxxx

Website (APA):

Organisation. (Year, Month Day). Page title. Site Name. URL

EE structure (suggested)

  • Title page • Abstract (if your school asks) • Contents
  • Introduction (context + RQ) • Method (data + tools) • Analysis (tables/graphs)
  • Discussion (limitations, alternatives) • Conclusion (answer the RQ)
  • References • Appendices (survey, raw tables)

IA structure (quick)

  • Econ IA: Article summary → diagrams → analysis with concepts → evaluation → references.
  • BM IA/HL: Research question → tools/frameworks (e.g., SWOT, investment appraisal) → findings → conclusion & recommendation → bibliography.
