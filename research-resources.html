Find quality sources

  • Google Scholar: use quotes, year filters, filetype:pdf, sort by date.
  • Official stats: World Bank, OECD, IMF, UN.
  • Open journals: DOAJ lists peer-reviewed OA journals.
  • Evaluate quality: author, publication, date, method, sample size, possible bias.

  • Capture the permalink or DOI, not just Google’s results URL.
  • Keep page numbers for quotes/paraphrases.

Referencing styles (IB rule)

IB accepts any recognized style (APA, MLA, Chicago) if used consistently.

APA (7th) quick examples
Article: Author, A. A. (Year). Title. Journal Name, volume(issue), pages. https://doi.org/xx.xxxx/xxxxx
Website: Author/Org. (Year, Month Day). Title. Site. URL
MLA (9th) quick examples
Article: Author Last, First. "Title." Journal vol., no., Year, pp.–pp. DOI/URL
Website: Author/Org. "Title." Site, Day Month Year, URL

In-text citations

  • APA: (Author, Year) or Author (Year).
  • MLA: (Author Page) or Author writes… (Page).
  • Paraphrase with a citation; quote sparingly; link every claim to a source.

EE/IA structure templates

  • EE (≈4,000 words): Title • Abstract (if required) • Contents • Introduction (RQ, context) • Method • Results • Analysis • Discussion • Conclusion • References • Appendices.
  • Econ IA: Article summary → labelled diagrams → analysis in context → evaluation → references.
  • BM IA/HL: Research question → tools/frameworks → findings → conclusion & recommendation → bibliography.
