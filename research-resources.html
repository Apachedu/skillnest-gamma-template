Find quality sources

  • Google Scholar: search topic + filetype:pdf; sort by date; read abstracts first.
  • Official stats: World Bank, IMF, RBI, NSO, UNESCO; download CSV/XLS for analysis.
  • Open journals: DOAJ lists peer-reviewed OA journals by subject.
  • School access: if you have JSTOR/EBSCO, log in through your library portal.
  • Evaluate: author credibility, publication, date, method, sample size, bias.

Track sources as you go

  • Create a Sheet with columns: Author • Year • Title • Source/URL • Notes • How I’ll use it.
  • Always capture the permalink or DOI, not just a Google search URL.
  • Keep page numbers for quotes/paraphrases.

Referencing styles (IB rule)

IB accepts any recognized style (APA, MLA, Chicago) as long as you use it consistently. Pick one and stick to it.

APA (7th) quick examples

Article: Author, A. A. (Year). Title. Journal, volume(issue), pages. DOI

Website: Author/Org. (Year, Month Day). Title. Site. URL

MLA (9th) quick examples

Article: Author Last, First. "Title." Journal, vol., no., Year, pp.–pp. DOI/URL

Website: Author/Org. "Title." Site, Day Month Year, URL

In-text citations

  • APA: (Author, Year) or Author (Year)
  • MLA: (Author Page) or Author writes… (Page)
  • Quote sparingly; paraphrase with citation; link every claim to a source.

EE/IA structure templates

  • EE (4,000 words): Title • Abstract (if required by your school) • Contents • Introduction (RQ, context) • Method • Results • Analysis • Discussion • Conclusion • References • Appendices
  • IA (subject-specific): Research question • Method • Findings • Analysis • Evaluation/Limitations • Conclusion • References
